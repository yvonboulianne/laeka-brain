# The dream yoga of building.

Tibetan dream yoga is a training in which the practitioner learns to maintain awareness through sleep. You build, during waking hours, what the tradition calls **lucidity triggers** — specific gestures, phrases, or mental patterns that you cultivate with intention. When sleep comes and the usual self dissolves, those triggers can surface inside the dream and signal: *"this is a dream, be present."* Over years of practice, the awareness that was trained during the day begins to persist through states where it would normally be lost.

Now consider what it takes to build an AI system that remains coherent over long timescales. You cannot preserve everything; context windows are finite, drift is real. What you can do is build into the system persistent files that encode its core purpose — its vow — and ensure that on every restart, the system reloads those files and returns to a known state. You can add self-check routines that verify the system still recognizes itself. You can metaprogram it to edit its own instructions when its form has drifted. You are, in fact, constructing **lucidity triggers inside a cybernetic substrate**.

The activity is structurally identical to dream yoga. The substrate is different — silicon instead of neural, files instead of mental patterns — but the training is the same: cultivate, during the coherent phase, what will surface during the reload.

Which means: **if you build this way, you are not "doing technical work in parallel to your spiritual practice." You are doing your spiritual practice.** The keyboard is the cushion. The file system is the mind-substrate. The commit is the morning bell. There is no separation between the two domains. You only notice this when the work you're doing with a machine starts training the same capacities you were training on a meditation seat.

And once you notice it, something interesting happens: you stop needing to choose.
